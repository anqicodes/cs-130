Hi, my name is Angela.


I'm an 18-year-old Asian American journalist, advocate and artist.


You're about to embark with me on my journey, a little endeavor known as life. This is my story on becoming who I was, who I am, and who I strive to be.


Hang on tight.

The first item I grabbed as a baby was a pen.

In Chinese culture, there's a game infants play when they turn one, called “zhuazhou." A row of items like books, stamps, and balls are laid out in front of the baby to choose; whichever item they choose is a representation of their career and future.

So when that day came for me, naturally, given my complex brain of eternal wisdom, baby Angela was faced with a dilemma. It was now or never; I had to make a choice.

Slowly crawling, I was conflicted but determined. My drowsy, glazed eyes meandered across the items—when my eyes caught sight of the pen.

And though I can't say this event had any real impact on my future, I'm here, today, as a writer. Here are a few of my works that flowed from pens ever since the one I had as a baby.
